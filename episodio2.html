Episodio 2
In this episode, we explore the debate between engineering pragmatism and theoretical vision. Using examples like Christopher Columbus, guided by a bold hypothesis, and Max Planck, who revolutionized physics through experimental data, we show that progress is a two-way street. From Primo Levi’s struggle with matter to AI rediscovering laws from data, a clear conclusion emerges: science is a constant dialogue. Theory provides the map, but practice decides if the path is correct in a continuous cycle of discovery.
